1. DMCA Notice Submission

This section explains the DMCA, why it is important, and how you can submit a takedown notice if you believe your copyright has been infringed.

Submitting a Takedown Notice

To submit a DMCA takedown notice, please include the following information:

  • A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or someone authorized to act on their behalf;
  • Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed;
  • Identification of the infringing material and information reasonably sufficient to permit the location of the material;
  • Your contact information, including your country of residence, and an email address;
  • A statement by you that you have a good faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law;
  • A statement that the information in the notification is accurate, and, under penalty of perjury, that you are authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed.

2. What happens after a DMCA notice is submitted

After a notice has been submitted we review the notice to ensure it meets all legal requirements and then act accordingly to remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material.
